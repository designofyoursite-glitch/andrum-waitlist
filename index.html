What Andrum is

Anchor the body. Allow the wave to move. Align with the next choice.

What You Get

The 7 practices

Practice 01
When Anxiety Hits After Messaging or a Conversation

A message came - or didn't. The pause felt too long. Something in your chest tightened before you even finished reading. This practice brings you back to your body when your nervous system fires before your mind can catch up. Breath by breath.

Practice 02
When Thoughts Are Racing and Won't Stop

You're replaying the conversation. Imagining what comes next. Trying to stop the loop - and somehow making it louder. You don't need to figure anything out right now. Just come back to your body. That's all this practice asks.

Practice 03
When I Feel Small, Wrong, or "Not Enough"

The inner voice is loud today. It says you got it wrong, you're too much, you don't belong. This practice doesn't silence that voice. It creates space beside it - warm, steady - where you can simply be. Exactly as you are right now.

Practice 04
When Jealousy Rises and You're Scared of Losing Them

The body is tense. Breathing is short. There's a knot inside. Jealousy isn't a flaw - it's a signal that you want to be seen. This practice helps it move through you, not take over. The longer exhale tells your body: you are safe right now.

Practice 05
When You're Desperate to Reach Out - Let's Walk

Something is burning. The urge to text, to fix it, to know - right now. Don't fight it. Let's walk it out instead. Put your earphones in and move. Breath and steps, together. The message can wait. This moment cannot.

Practice 06
When I Freeze and Feel Nothing

Not sadness. Not anxiety. Just... nothing. A heavy absence where feeling used to be. This practice doesn't force anything. It gently warms the body back - layer by layer, breath by breath - the way you'd thaw something slowly from the inside out.

Practice 07
When Anger Rises and You Want to Tell Everyone Off

The heat is rising. You want to say something sharp and you know you'll regret it. Anger isn't the enemy - it's the alarm. This practice gives it somewhere to go. Loud, physical, real. The calm comes after. It always does.

Each audio is 5-10 minutes and designed for one specific state, so you do not have to decide what you need when you are already overwhelmed.

Why Breathwork

This is not about thinking your way out.

Anxious attachment is not only a thought pattern. It is a body state.

Your heart speeds up. Your breath changes. Your chest tightens. Your nervous system reads uncertainty as danger.

That is why advice, journaling, or "just calm down" often cannot reach you in the moment.

Andrum starts where the reaction starts: in the body.

Longer exhales help signal safety to the nervous system.

Structured breathing gives the mind something steady to follow.

Movement helps metabolise urgency and activation.

A guided voice holds the rhythm when you cannot hold it alone.

How It Works

Anchor · Allow · Align

Anchor

Come back to the body.

Allow

Open the space.

The goal is not to become someone who never gets triggered. The goal is to find the way back faster.

Who It's For

This is for you if...

  • You feel calm on the outside, but activated inside close relationships.
  • You replay messages, silences, tones, and tiny shifts.
  • You understand your patterns, but still cannot stop the body reaction.
  • You reach for reassurance and then feel ashamed for needing it.
  • You want a tool for the exact moment, not another concept to think about.

Andrum is not a replacement for therapy. It is a practical support tool for the moments between sessions, conversations, and choices.
